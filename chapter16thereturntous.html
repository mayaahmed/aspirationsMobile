Chapter 16

The Return to America

Arti bought jasmine flower garlands ever day. Jasmine flower garlands was one of the reason Arti had wanted to come back to India. She had missed Jasmine flowers. Arti and Kabir also visited a lot of spiritual places. The temples somehow felt live and real as though gods really lived there.

"So many major religions of the world originated in India" said Arti. "Every one can experience spirituality here."

"The place seems spiritual because the people are so" said Kabir.

The couple visited many temples where people went to make wishes come true. Arti had no desire to make wishes. She was happy to be there at that moment of time in a temple. On the one hand one could wish for the world and on the other one could wish for nothing. Arti realized that it made no difference. This was her land. She belonged here. Everything made sense. The Indian life made her happy.

There was a little remorse of the innocence she had lost in her travels. The poverty of India jarred now that Arti was more aware of it after her travels. Looking around India, a question repeated in Arti's mind. Why were the lives of so many people so harsh? It was just a matter of fate that she was not one of those.

"India's poverty is pathological" said Arti. "It is not normal for any country to be so poor. Of course there is the British factor but I do not see why things are in such a state. Isn't it strange that we have divided the society so clearly into the haves and have nots? What strikes me most is that the poor people do not seem real to me. It is like I have shut myself from thinking about them. Is that the way we can go on with life without becoming morbid?"

"That is kind of true" replied Kabir. "The poor people live within themselves as a group. They behave to people outside the group differently. If they see a rich person they either want to extract money from them or be extra nice to prove that they may be poor but can be quite generous."

"All societies are fundamentally feudal and the people from a certain class keep to themselves" said Arti.

"Seems to be so" agreed Kabir.

"I am not sure I am not guilty. For some reason, I have a feeling that I am a part of all this economic exploitation" said Arti.

Time and again Arti felt that a way to contribute towards eradication of poverty was by sharing personal wealth. Not to a charity institution but directly towards a cause. Personal involvement or what some people would call getting your hands dirty was the only way to get results. But she was still a student that too without any funding. All noble causes had to wait for now.

Moreover, the Indian society was going through a conflict period. Every time there was a street fight between people of two different communities the whole country would be dragged in. There would be riots. Houses would burn and people would get killed.

"Do not go today to visit your parents" said Kabir. "Bangalore is volatile at this point. There was some stone throwing at the bus stop."

Arti was disappointed.

"What happened?" asked Arti.

"Two young people had a motorcycle accident and the fight percolated" said Kabir.

"I really hate being dragged in to a fight relating to a motorcycle accident that has nothing to do with me" said Arti really irritated.

That was the rule. You were dragged in to all kinds of fights and your plans would get disrupted. Arti began to miss America. There were no conflicts in America. Moreover Sharma left India and Arti had no one to work with. America was hiring big time because the economy was booming. Kabir kept getting job offers from America all the time. Arti and Kabir realized that they had moved back to India a little early. Arti should have finished her PhD and Kabir should have accumulated enough money to buy a good house. The inevitable happened. Soon Kabir got a job offer he could not refuse. The couple flew back to America, a land where all dreams came true quite easily.
