Chapter 10

Flying home to India

Arti was flying back to India from America with her husband Kabir. It was the nineties. Getting visas to live in America was difficult. The couple had just left a dream life of millions of people living in India who would do anything to go to America. It had been a good stay in America. There were plenty of opportunities and good money to be made. Kabir held a dream job and Arti went to the best of schools. But there was a nagging discontent. The couple always felt that there was something wrong about leaving India for a better life. It was as though they were neglecting an old parent. They made plans to go back to India often. And finally they took the plunge and here they were heading back to the world they had left behind six years ago.

"Maybe I denied myself the right to miss India" said Kabir. "Only when I begin to go towards India that I realize that how much I have missed home and India."

"We lose ourselves in work to avoid the fact that we are missing out on real life" said Arti. "Many times we don't realize it. The rich web of relations and emotions is essential to make life meaningful. Though I may not be up to facing the emotional turmoils of everyday life in India."

Arti and Kabir were young hot tickets with nothing to worry about. Opportunities to pick and choose were assured wherever they went. Kabir was taking up a job in Bangalore and Arti would continue her studies. But first she would go home to her parents. Her family was there at the airport to meet her. It had been six long years. She really had missed them big time. This was like a dream and Arti smiled to herself. Six years and she had not seen these faces she loved so much. When she got home she did not feel like doing anything. She just sat and watched everyone buzzing around. Many things had changed and it took getting used to. Her pet dog had died while she was away. Her little brother was married. Her parents had moved to a new house. A lot of things were different now including herself.

Neighbors came to visit her. These were people she had known as a child.

"How are you? How is America?" asked Masi.

"Fine, fine" said Arti.

Words failed her but she was happy to see Masi. Arti's mother bought out some chocolates and a few other things and put it in a small bag and gave it to Masi.

"You must take these chocolates for the children. Arti brought this from America" said Arti's mother.

Masi was quite happy with the little packet she got. Masi asked Arti

"Where is your husband?"

"He will be here today" replied Arti's mother.

"Oh, that is why you are so busy preparing so many things?" asked Masi.

"Yes. After all one has to treat one's son in law well" said Arti's mother.

"That is true. That is true. So you will settle down in Mumbai?" asked Masi.

"No, they are going to Bangalore" said Arti's mother.

"That is too bad. You should settle down in Mumbai. Then you can visit home more often" said Masi.

"It is better than America. I can see my child more often now" said Arti's mother.

Arti's mother was answering all the questions for her but Arti did not mind. She just sat smiling at everybody and she felt very happy.

"I am so happy to be home" said Arti again. She found herself saying this line over and over again. "I have met more people in two days than all the people I met in America in six years."

It was strange to find her little brother married. He was entertaining his friends like a grown man. For Arti he remained a little boy but she had to show deference in front of his wife. Arti's mother offered tea to the visitors but they were hesitant since it was a hot day.

"I will make you some herbal tea" said Arti all too happy to introduce people to something new. Not many people drank herbal tea in India. Now she would introduce it to the little crowd gathered in their living room.

Arti made some black tea, poured it in to fancy cups, and offered it to everyone. If Arti had a little doubt that people would not like the herbal tea she made then she was not wrong at all. People were making all sorts of contorted faces after the first sip they drank.

"How can you call this tea? This tastes more like medicine" someone said.

"Hey, this is the way hundreds of people drink tea in America. You just need an open mind to enjoy it. Now try another sip. The first sip is always a surprise. It is all a matter of getting used to a new taste" said Arti now certain that she had given them the necessary psychological setup to enjoy something new like herbal tea. But the verdict was that herbal tea was no good. In fact the subject of herbal tea became a topic of great mirth for a few weeks after that day. One just had to say herbal tea and Arti's brother's friends would begin laughing.

"It was my mistake trying to introduce you junglees to world cuisine. See whether I will ever try that again" said Arti angrily.

Time seemed to fly and soon it was time to go to Bangalore.

"It is one journey after the other. Bangalore is as foreign to me as America" whispered Arti to Kabir in the plane.

"One day we will settle down. But now we must explore and see the world. And with you beside me, I feel I am always home" said Kabir smiling at her.

The plane food was excellent. It was food she understood.

"It is nice to know the history of what you are eating" said Arti. "This vada-pao in the tray is street food of Mumbai though it is stylishly presented when served in the plane. If you did not know this, it did not matter much. But it just good to know what you know" said Arti.

"You know these details only if you are Indian. We know a lot about India. It is only when you have been away that you realize it" said Kabir.

When they landed in Bangalore Kabir's company people were waiting for him with his name written on a welcome sign board. They offered Arti a big bouquet of flowers and took them to the motel they were going to stay in. It was the best motel in all of Bangalore.

"Next week we will hunt for a house. Tomorrow I will go to the office and see what it is like" said Kabir.

They sat in the balcony of the motel and watched the sunset. It was a new life they were facing together again.

"I am at at peace in India" said Arti.

"People are made of the sand, air, and water of the place they live in" said Kabir. "We are in sync in the place we were born. Hence we are at peace here."

"The bible says that you are dust and to dust thou shall return" said Arti. "It makes sense. The plants create food out of sand, air, and water. The food chain begins with plants and thus we are made of the sand, air, and water of the land we live in."

"By that logic we are made of the sand, air, and water of all the places we visit" said Kabir. "Some of us are made of international sand, air, and water."

But for Arti six years of American soil was dominated by decades of Indian soil.

"There was India with me wherever I went" said Arti. "I created a little India everywhere I went around me. But being in India is different. When it is my turn to return to dust it is India's dust I want to return to."
