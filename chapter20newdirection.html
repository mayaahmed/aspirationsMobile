Chapter 20

Seeking new Roads

Arti's old school offered her a temporary teaching position. It was painful to go back to teach mathematics. She did not consider herself a success story. She was having a hard time. Perhaps she would never ask another student to become a mathematician. Or perhaps she would bring about enough changes so that she could tell her students again that it was cool to be a mathematician. Only time would tell. For now, it was wise to avoid the question altogether.

Arti had not realized when she signed up for the job that the pay would be so low. It was almost the same as she got as a graduate student but now there was no subsidized housing. There was no way any one could survive on this kind of pay in California. But she signed up for it and her students were waiting. So she decided to finish this assignment.

Instead of traveling by train three hours each way to teach, waking up at unearthly hours, Arti decided to stay at a friends place. This was so opposed to her nature but that was the best option. But it turned out to be a good idea. Arti really got to know her friend. Many of the safeguards a person adopts when one is outside the house is no longer there and a real bond is possible. It is also true that the awe you might have for another person diminishes considerably too. In a work environment a person might have skills that no one else has but at home the person is like everyone else. Arti's friend had come a long way from a small village in India to California. She had many interesting stories to tell.

Arti stayed at her friend's place every night before she taught a class. Arti cooked and packed some dinner for the night. Her friend made some delicious meals. They shared their food and had long conversations. Arti really had a good time.

One of the lessons she learned after her PhD was that it was alright to ask for help sometimes. The recent trip to India had taught her this too. People in India did not always have too much money. But the culture of sharing and helping was what kept them going at all odd times. Keeping one's self pride intact was always a big factor but friends and relatives were also quite important.

By now, the US economy had slipped down further. Arti's friends were all searching for jobs. To her amusement Arti realized that every time she was unhappy there were many unhappy people around her. Was that when she was unhappy, other people opened up about their own problems? Moreover, not many people had an agenda of spreading joy in the world. Finally the teaching assignment came to an end. It was satisfying to see that students had studied hard and learned something.

"Look my book is in tatters" a student pointed out to her.

Arti smiled and said

"It is a challenging subject. You need to work hard."

Arti knew that not many teachers could teach like her. But she would not return to teach, not for this pittance of a pay. It was a myth that mathematicians are smart if they were willing to work so hard for this kind of money.

"Women do not get along well" said Arti. "They say that Laxmi, the goddess of wealth, and Saraswati, the goddess of knowledge, will not work together. Hence one has to choose between money and knowledge."

"Unless you are Kalidas" said Kabir laughing.

"What about Kalidas?" asked Arti.

"He tricked the goddess Kali to grant him both wealth and knowledge" said Kabir.

"I will emulate Kalidas" said Arti.

It was like Arti had woken up from slumber. A new path she would find for herself. It now surprised her that she even wanted to spend a lifetime teaching mathematics. An era of uncertainty was looming large. The state of academics seemed obsolete. The very purpose of academic institutions would be to encourage critical thinking and hold varied opinions. Education was getting more and more specialized and the broader picture was getting lost. Change was imminent for academics and for Arti. She now focused on engineering.
